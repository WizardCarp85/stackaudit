# Go-To-Market Strategy

### The Exact Target User
The target user is not "a startup founder." The exact user is a **VP of Finance or Fractional CFO** at a Series A or B startup (roughly 50 to 150 employees, $10M–$50M in funding). At this stage, the startup has outgrown the "buy whatever tools you need" phase, and the finance leader is explicitly tasked with extending the runway by auditing runaway software spend.

### What They Google Right Before Wanting This
Before finding StackAudit, this user is frustratedly scrolling through their Brex or Ramp dashboard. They are Googling highly specific, pain-driven queries like:
* "How to audit SaaS spend startup"
* "GitHub Copilot vs Cursor enterprise pricing overlap"
* "Average AI tool spend per engineer 2026"
* "Cancel unused ChatGPT Plus seats automatically"

### Where They Hang Out Online
They do not hang out on Twitter or IndieHackers. They are in private, vetted finance communities:
* **Slack Groups:** "Off the Ledger" (run by Airbase, massive CFO community), "CFO Connect", and "Secret CFO" private groups.
* **Newsletters/Forums:** They read "The SaaS CFO" newsletter and hang out in r/fpanda (FP&A professionals on Reddit) asking for spreadsheet templates to track SaaS sprawl.

### First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 Budget)
"Post on Twitter" won't work. Here is the highly specific, zero-dollar playbook:
1. **The Crunchbase to LinkedIn Pipeline:** I will use the free tier of Crunchbase to filter for startups that raised a Series A exactly 9 to 12 months ago (this is when board pressure to cut burn usually hits). I will cross-reference LinkedIn to find their VP of Finance.
2. **The "Cold Value" Email:** I will send a highly personalized cold email: *"Hey [Name], saw you raised your Series A last year. Board probably asking about burn right now. I built a free tool that finds hidden AI subscription overlap (like engineers expensing both Claude and ChatGPT). It takes 3 minutes and doesn't require API access. Want the link?"*
3. **The "Roast My Stack" Slack Strategy:** I will join the "Off the Ledger" Slack channel and offer a free service: *"Post your engineering tool list, and I'll manually run it through my AI overlap calculator and tell you how much you are wasting."* I will use StackAudit to generate the result, then reply with a link to the tool.

### The Unfair Distribution Channel
The one thing only Credex can do is **inject StackAudit into the loan underwriting process**. When a startup applies for credit financing or venture debt through Credex, the application process should require them to run StackAudit. We pitch it as: *"Run this free 3-minute audit to instantly qualify for lower financing rates by proving your software stack is optimized."* This guarantees high-intent, immediate traffic from our exact target persona.

### Week-1 Traction Targets
If this playbook works, Week 1 looks exactly like this:
* **Unique Visitors:** 350 (driven largely by direct outbound and Slack links).
* **Audits Completed:** 70 (a 20% completion rate).
* **Emails Captured:** 15.
* **Consultations Booked:** 2 qualified calls with the Credex sales team.
